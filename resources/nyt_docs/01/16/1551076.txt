Concerts featuring works by Schumann are planned by the New York Philharmonic in the coming months. Unless noted, those concerts are at Avery Fisher Hall. Tickets: $32 to $114; information, (212) 875-5656. Four all-Schumann concerts at Carnegie Hall featuring the Staatskapelle Berlin, conducted by Daniel Barenboim, begin on Wednesday; tickets: $29 to $137; Carnegie Hall information: (212) 247-7800. A schedule of the Philharmonic and Carnegie Hall programs, as well as a sampling of other Schumann concerts:
Today
NEW YORK PHILHARMONIC, conducted by Kurt Masur, Performing Arts Center, Purchase College, Anderson Hill Road, Purchase, N.Y. Schumann's Symphony No. 3; Weber's ''Freischütz'' Overture; Shostakovich's Violin Concerto No. 1, Sarah Chang, violinist. 8 p.m.; tickets, $45, $60 and $75. Information: (914) 251-6200.
Tomorrow and Tuesday
NEW YORK PHILHARMONIC, Avery Fisher Hall. Same program as above. 8 p.m. tomorrow; Tuesday at 7:30 p.m.
Wednesday
STAATSKAPELLE BERLIN, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's Piano Concerto in A minor and Symphony No. 2 in C; Radu Lupu, pianist. 8 p.m.
Next Friday
STAATSKAPELLE BERLIN, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's ''Manfred'' Overture; the Cello Concerto; and the Symphony No. 1; Yo-Yo Ma, cellist, 8 p.m.
Jan. 24
STAASKAPELLE BERLIN, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's Violin Concerto, with Gidon Kremer as soloist, and the Symphony No. 4 in D minor, 8 p.m. A preconcert talk at 7 p.m.

STAATSKAPELLE BERLIN, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's Symphony No. 3; Introduction and Allegro Appassionato in G major and Introduction and Concert-Allegro in D minor and D major, with Jonathan Biss as pianist, 2 p.m.

STEPHANE DEGOUT, baritone, Walter Reade Theater, Lincoln Center. Schumann's ''Belsatzar'' and ''Die Beiden Grenadiere'' and works by Debussy, Ravel, Strauss, Brahms and Duparc. 7:30 p.m. Tickets, $25. Information: (212) 721-6500 or (212) 875-5050.

IVAN MORAVEC, pianist, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's ''Kinderszenen''; and works by Beethoven, Brahms and Debussy. 8 p.m. Tickets, $22 to $69.

Schumann's ''Spanisches Liederspiel'' (Op. 74 and 138); and Brahms's ''Liebeslieder-Walzer.'' 7:30 p.m. Tickets, $30.

MIKHAIL PLETNEV, pianist, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's ''Fünf Albumblätter'' from ''Bunte Blätter'' and Fantasy in C; Tchaikovsky's 18 Pieces. 8 p.m. Tickets, $25 to $84.

CONCERTO KÖLN, conducted by David Stern, Carnegie Hall. Schumann's Cello Concerto and works by Mendelssohn and Wagner. Jean-Guihen Queyras, cellist. 8 p.m. Tickets, $24 to $79.

June 10 to 12
NEW YORK PHILHARMONIC, conducted by Lorin Maazel; Schumann's Symphony No. 2; Profofiev's Violin Concerto No. 2; Poul Ruders's ''Final Nightshade''; Gil Shaham, violinist, 7:30 p.m. on June 10; 2 p.m. on June 11; 8 p.m. on June 12.
