A day after scolding member states for their lack of commitment to reforms, the European Commission said it was taking eight countries to the European Court of Justice for failing to enact a European Union directive on the insurance industry. The directive was intended to safeguard policyholders if an insurance company collapsed. The law accepted by all 15 member states in 2001 was supposed to have been made national law by last April.
Paul Meller (NYT)
