The trade deficit staged an eightfold increase in November compared with the same month a year ago, to $322 million, according to government statistics. Imports were driven up by electronics components, most of which are subsequently used to assemble other electronics products and then exported. While some economists said they suspected exports might have been underreported, others said the new data underscored the weakness of the Philippine economy compared with its Asian neighbors.
Wayne Arnold (NYT)
