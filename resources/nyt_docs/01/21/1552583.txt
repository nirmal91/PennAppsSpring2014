The police charged two young men yesterday in connection with the fatal shooting of a Bronx man early Monday morning aboard a subway train in Greenwich Village. Ortillio Tapia, 21, of the Bronx, was charged with murder and criminal possession of a weapon, and Pedro Revilla, 17, of Manhattan, was charged with criminal possession of a weapon. The police said that Mr. Revilla boarded a northbound W train at the Eighth Street station with the victim, Silverio Rivera, 25, just before 4 a.m. on Monday. The two of them came across Mr. Tapia; a fight ensued, with Mr. Tapia and Mr. Revilla drawing guns, and Mr. Tapia shot Mr. Rivera twice in the head, the police said. The police said that there were a few other passengers in the subway car at the time, but that none were hurt. Mr. Rivera was taken to Bellevue Hospital Center, where he died.
