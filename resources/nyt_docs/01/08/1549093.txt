Objections from Beijing have derailed plans to set a timetable for democratic changes, senior Hong Kong officials said as they announced a committee to begin meetings with mainland government departments. Officials had promised to set a timetable by the end of last month, but Donald Tsang, the second-ranking Hong Kong government official, said that President Hu Jintao of China expressed concerns to Tung Chee-hwa, Hong Kong's chief executive, and that Beijing officials subsequently asked for consultations.
