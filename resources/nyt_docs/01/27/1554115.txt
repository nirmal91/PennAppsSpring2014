Japan's fourth-largest lender, UFJ Holdings, canceled a planned bond sale amid reports financial regulators are investigating the way the bank classifies its nonperforming loans. UFJ, which denied a report in Nihon Keizai Shimbun saying regulators would begin an investigation, nonetheless postponed the sale of bonds worth $677 million. Regulators at the Financial Services Agency declined to comment on the report and the bank did not set a new date for the sale.
