An Indian software services company, Wipro, said its quarterly profit rose 18.6 percent, reflecting an increase in outsourcing of technology and related work to India by American corporations. Wipro, which is based in Bangalore, said it earned 2.74 billion rupees ($61 million) in the quarter, which ended in December. Azim Premji, chairman of Wipro, above, said its results were helped by an increase in volume and stable prices.
