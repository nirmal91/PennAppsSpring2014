Something good can probably be said about every human. Melanie Thernstrom not only fails to look for it in the life of this one man, Jack Silecchia, but she also clearly relies for the totality of her account on his widow, who rightly or wrongly detested him. Since he will not be accorded any other obituary in a newspaper like The Times, this is the only image of him that thousands of readers will ever see.
Richard Alba  Albany
