The major stock indexes climbed to 2004 highs last week as earnings reports from the fourth quarter of last year struck a positive note and new data showed that consumer confidence had reached a three-year high.
The Standard &amp; Poor's 500-stock index rose 17.97 points, or 1.6 percent, to 1,139.83, and is now at its highest level since April 2002. It has climbed for eight consecutive weeks.
The Nasdaq composite index climbed 53.54 points, or 2.6 percent, to 2,140.46, for its sixth week of gains and its highest close since July 2001. The Dow Jones industrial average rose 141.62 points, or 1.4 percent, finishing at 10,600.51, its best close since March 2002; the Dow has risen for eight consecutive weeks.
I.B.M. jumped 4.5 percent after it reported better-than-expected earnings and said that executives were optimistic about the outlook. General Electric rose 4.2 percent Friday after it announced that fourth-quarter earnings rose 47 percent. Intel and Apple Computer also reported strong gains in quarterly net income, but both stocks were lower at the end of the week.
In the bond market, the yield on the Treasury's 10-year note slipped to 4.02 percent, from 4.08 percent a week earlier.
