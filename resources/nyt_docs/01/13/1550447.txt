The electronics and engineering company Siemens said it had agreed to sell half of its stake in Infineon Technologies, Europe's second-largest chip maker, for about 1.8 billion euros ($2.3 billion). Siemens, based in Munich, said it had agreed to sell 150 million shares as part of its plan to divest itself of all  its Infineon stake. Stock in Infineon, which is also based in Munich, fell more than 5 percent on the news that Siemens was reducing its stake to 19 percent.
