In Matt Bai's article (Jan. 18), we read that sophisticated marketing tools like focus groups help pollsters figure out what ''buttons'' to push to manipulate voters. What if the candidate ignored polls, just acted naturally, addressing the major issues straightforwardly. That couldn't be any riskier than the present strategy of trying to court voters with personality transformations and budget-bloating campaign promises that fool almost no one.
Edwin P. Berlin  San Diego
