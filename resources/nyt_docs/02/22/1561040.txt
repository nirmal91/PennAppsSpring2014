I'm a reader, but I'm also a journalist, and I have the feeling you're going to be setting judicial precedent for our profession.
Your Dec. 21 ruling on Barry Meier and the drug company Purdue Pharma suggests that a journalist should stop covering a subject after he writes a book about it. Your stance would discourage journalists from pursuing their interests at book length and deprive newspapers of their expertise if they did.
If a book extends a journalist's scrupulous coverage of a story, does it matter that much that he's being paid royalties instead of a salary?
BROOKE UNGER  São Paulo, Brazil, Dec. 21, 2003
The writer is São Paulo bureau chief for The Economist.
