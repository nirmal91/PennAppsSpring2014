Harris Bank of Chicago, a unit of the Bank of Montreal, has agreed to buy New Lenox State Bank of New Lenox, Ill., for $228.5 million as it expands in retail and small-business services in the Chicago area. New Lenox operates eight branches, with assets of $998 million. The Bank of Montreal has made 13 acquisitions in the United States in the last four years. With the New Lenox purchase, Harris will operate a total of 163 branches and 450 automated teller machines.
