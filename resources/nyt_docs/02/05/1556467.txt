Colombia's grinding internal conflict has generated up to three million internally displaced refugees, a crisis that is one of the world's worst but that has been largely ignored, a senior United Nations official said in Geneva after visiting Colombia and its porous borders. The official, Kamel Morjane, assistant high commissioner for refugees, said the international community should do more to help displaced Colombians, and he called the crisis second in scope only to the ones in Congo and Sudan.
Juan Forero (NYT)
