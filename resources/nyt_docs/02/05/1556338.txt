In Their Own Words  American Folk Art Museum, 45 West 53rd Street; (212) 265-1040.
''Talking Quilts,'' an exhibition exploring the use of words in textiles since the early 19th century, opens on Wednesday and runs through Aug. 1. Included are an 1848 quilt with the motto ''If you can't be a golden pippin, don't turn crab apple,'' and a 1998 work by Robin Schwalb with stenciled lines of dialogue from the television program ''Homicide: Life on the Street.''
One of the quilters, Kyra Hicks, will speak about African-American quilting on Feb. 21 at 1:30 p.m., followed by a workshop, at 3 p.m., on telling stories with quilts (reservations required). The fee for the lecture is $10 (members, students and those 65 and older, $7); with the workshop, $15 (members, students and those 65 and older, $12). Robin Schwalb will speak on March 5 at 6 p.m. The fee is $10 (members, students and those 65 and older, $7).
A second exhibition, ''Tools of Her Ministry: The Art of Sister Gertrude Morgan,'' a retrospective, will run from Feb. 25 through Sept. 26. It will include 100 paintings and painted objects made between 1956 and 1976. The artist (1900-80) was a street evangelist in New Orleans known for work that frequently incorporated Scripture and portraits of herself in white costume.
The museum is open Wednesday through Sunday, 10:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. (until 7:30 p.m. on Friday). Admission is $9; students and those 65 and older, $7.

Architecture Past  Institute of Classical Architecture and Classical America; (212) 924-9686.
The institute, in conjunction with other organizations, is offering lectures about historic architecture. Reservations are required.
Kathleen Deegan, a curator at the Florida Museum of Natural History at the University of Florida, will offer an illustrated talk on Feb. 12 at 6:30 p.m. about La Isabela, the first coastal settlement on the island of Hispaniola. The lecture will be given at the New-York Historical Society, Central Park West at 77th Street. Admission is $7; students and those 65 and older, $4.
Two talks on architecture in Sweden will be offered at Scandinavia House, 58 Park Avenue (37th Street). One, on March 30 at 7 p.m., will look at the 17th and 18th centuries; the 19th and 20th centuries will be the subject of the second program, on April 13 at 7 p.m. Admission to each lecture, including a reception at 6:30 p.m., $30; members, $25.
