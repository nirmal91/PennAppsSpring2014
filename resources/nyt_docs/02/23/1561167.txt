E. Phillips Hathaway, a retired Maryland investment banker and a foundation executive, died on Feb. 6 at his home in Owings Mills, Md. He was 83.
The cause was congestive heart failure, his family said.
At his death, Mr. Hathaway was president of the Middendorf Foundation of Baltimore. The foundation, with assets of more than $30 million, contributes to art museums, historical societies, hospices, libraries and Protestant agencies, among other causes.
Born in Boston, Edwin Phillips Hathaway was attending the University of Virginia when the United States entered World War II. He became a Marine Corps aviator, flying missions over Saipan, Iwo Jima and Japan. He earned three Air Medals and the Distinguished Flying Cross.
He joined Middendorf, Colgate &amp; Company, an investment-banking firm, in 1946, and at the direction of J. William Middendorf, Mr. Hathaway established the Middendorf Foundation. After the banking firm was dissolved in 1973, he was a partner in Investment Counselors of Maryland until he retired six years later.
Mr. Hathaway is survived by his wife of 58 years, Barbara Sealy Mallory Hathaway; three daughters, Mallory Hathaway of Manhattan, Rebecca H. Lynch of Stonington, Conn., and Sealy H. Hopkinson of Huntington, N.Y.; a son, Phillips, of Salisbury, Conn.; a brother, H. Grant, of Baltimore; and five grandchildren.
