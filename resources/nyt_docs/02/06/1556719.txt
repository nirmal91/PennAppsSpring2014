Thousands of ethnic-Russian students rallied in Riga, the capital, in the largest protest so far against a measure that would limit the use of Russian in the country's schools. The Parliament, brushing aside the protest, voted overwhelming to require that 60 percent of classes in public schools be taught in Latvian, even though Russian remains the first language of nearly a third of the country's 2.2 million residents.
Steven Lee Myers (NYT)
