An explosion tore through a subway train here during the morning rush hour on Friday, and there were reports of many casualties.
The cause was not immediately clear, but it appeared to be a terrorist act, the police said.
The explosion occurred at 8:45 a.m. in the second car of a subway train in southeast Moscow, not far from the city center, emergency service officials told news agencies. The car was reported to be badly damaged. The explosion ignited a large fire, the officials said, and smoke poured from the subway tunnels and entrances.
The blast happened as the train moved between the Paveletskaya and the Avtozavodskaya stations at one of the busiest times of the day. Dozens of ambulances and investigators converged on the scene.
