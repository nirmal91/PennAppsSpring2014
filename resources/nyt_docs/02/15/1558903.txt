Roger Lowenstein (Feb. 1) implies that the Rigases were mere rubes, misled and eventually done in by slickers from the big banks, big law firms and big accounting houses. While maybe not as unlikable as some other corporate executives mentioned in the article, the Rigas family apparently shared many other qualities: obfuscation, intimidation and a penchant for the bottomless checkbook.
Joe Salsberry  Summit, N.J.
